**Level:** High School (Grades 9–11) / Ages 14–17
**Prerequisite modules:** None (self-contained entry point)
**Standards alignment:** NSF Mathematics Grade 9–10, Physics Grade 10, CS Grade 9; UWI Department of Physics; Luminous.Works LLC

---

### Module Overview

Signal Physics is the curriculum track that connects the behaviour of physical waves to the mathematics of audio engineering. Students move from real historical events (resonance disasters) through the mathematics of power, logarithms, and threshold logic, arriving at the ability to read and reason about real C++ signal processing code.

**The central question of this module:** How do we protect physical and electronic systems from the forces they generate themselves?

### Diagram Requirements

The following visual aids have been flagged as required for non-technical audiences. A visual designer should be commissioned for these:

1. **The Five-Step Signal Flow** — Square → Average → Log → Threshold → Inverse Log. Should be a horizontal pipeline diagram with a sample value tracked through each stage.
2. **Feed-Forward vs. Feedback** — Two parallel signal chain diagrams showing where detection happens in each architecture.
3. **The Bridge Resonance Diagram** — Force amplitude over time showing the soldiers' footfall frequency matching the bridge's natural frequency, with the collapse point marked.
4. **dB Scale Visual** — Logarithmic ruler showing linear power on one axis, dB on the other. The "every factor of 10 = 10 dB" pattern made visual.
