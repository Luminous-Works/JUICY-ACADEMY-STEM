A Pristine Underground World

Green Grotto represents one of the Caribbean's most intact cave ecosystems. The system contains multiple chambers, vast underground lakes of extraordinary clarity, and geological formations untouched by modern disturbance.

  • Underground lakes: Multiple freshwater bodies with unique geochemistry and biology
  • Speleothems: Stalactites, stalagmites, and flowstone formations spanning millennia
  • Guano ecosystems: Bat and bird populations supporting cave-dependent food webs
  • Water clarity: Exceptional transparency indicating pristine aquatic conditions
  • Archaeological layers: Undisturbed stratigraphic records of Taíno occupation

JUICY's real-time monitoring ensures this pristine system is protected while its water quality, ecological integrity, and archaeological significance are scientifically documented for UNESCO World Heritage inscription.

UNESCO World Heritage Designation

Green Grotto meets UNESCO's most stringent criteria for World Heritage protection:

  • Geological processes demonstrating cave formation over geological timescales
  • Archaeological significance as evidence of pre-Columbian Caribbean civilization
  • Hydrological importance as a pristine freshwater resource system
  • Ecological integrity as a complete cave ecosystem
  • Exceptional universal value combining multiple criteria of World Heritage significance

JUICY's scientific framework transforms Green Grotto from a locally known site into internationally documented and protected heritage. International designation ensures permanent protection for this irreplaceable sanctuary.
