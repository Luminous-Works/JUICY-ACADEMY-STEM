## II. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: THE PROBLEM JAMAICA FACES

Jamaica currently operates with critical infrastructure gaps that leave the nation vulnerable:

### **Education Gap**
- STEM curriculum lacks integration with real-world applications and government operations
- Students learn theory in classrooms; practice is disconnected from national priorities
- Jamaica lacks a centralized, world-class, college-ready STEM education platform
- Regional disadvantage: Other Caribbean nations are implementing integrated educational technology

### **Emergency Response Gap**
- Hurricane preparedness relies on external forecasts (US National Weather Service) with 6+ hour local lag
- No real-time integration of environmental data (water quality, sargassum, coastal conditions)
- Interagency coordination is manual (phone calls, email, conflicting data sets)
- Hospital capacity, port operations, transportation logistics lack real-time coordination
- Coastal communities receive generic US forecasts, not Jamaica-specific alerts

### **Data Sovereignty Gap**
- Jamaica's environmental and operational data is processed through foreign systems
- Decision-making relies on overseas gateways rather than local infrastructure
- No Caribbean-centric data infrastructure designed for Jamaica's specific geography and challenges
- Sargassum data: Jamaica appears in 3% of peer-reviewed research globally (Mexico 77%), indicating data ownership imbalance

**Result:** Jamaica operates its most critical functions (education and emergency response) with infrastructure gaps that a Caribbean nation should not tolerate.
