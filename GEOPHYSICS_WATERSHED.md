# GEOPHYSICS & WATERSHED: Jamaica Type-Locality Karst
## Module: GWS-400 (College-Level STEM / UWI Alignment)

### 1. Architectural Overview: The Karst "BlackBox"
The Jamaican landscape, particularly the **Cockpit Country**, serves as the global type-locality for polygonal karst. Understanding this environment requires a transition from surface-level observation to deep-crustal geophysics.

#### Key Formations:
*   **White Limestone Group**: The primary aquifer. High porosity, well-jointed, subject to intense dissolution.
*   **Yellow Limestone Group**: The aquiclude. Functions as a confining layer that dictates subterranean conduit orientation.

---

### 2. Geomorphological Sites
#### A. Cockpit Country (The Heart)
*   **Topography**: Polygonal karst featuring steep-sided conical hills and star-shaped depressions.
*   **Hydrology**: A vital national aquifer. Water moves through the **epikarst** and into vertical shafts (conduits).
*   **Lab Link**: Use **Fe·26 Iron** to monitor seismic stress indexes (σ) along the Duanvale fault system.

#### B. Green Grotto Caves (Subterranean)
*   **Speleogenesis**: Formed by structural joint control and sea-level fluctuation.
*   **Observation**: Active speleothem development and structural passage orientation (joint/fault influence).
*   **Lab Link**: Deploy **Sp·0 Spectre** for acoustic profiling of limestone densities and void identification.

#### C. Fern Gully (Structural Gorge)
*   **Origin**: Tectonic uplift of a former river bed.
*   **Geology**: High-exposure road-cuts providing a vertical cross-section of White Limestone stratigraphy.
*   **Lab Link**: Use **Sn·50 Tin** metallurgy logic to understand the oxidation and mineral weathering rates of the cliff faces.
