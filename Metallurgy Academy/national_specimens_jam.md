# JAMAICAN NATIONAL SPECIMENS & GEOLOGY
## Endemic Flora and Industrial Mineralogy

### 1. Blue Mahoe (Talipariti elatum)
*   **Status**: National Tree of Jamaica.
*   **Habitat**: Indigenous; common in higher altitudes (over 1000ft) with rich rainfall.
*   **Anatomy**: Characterized by beautiful blue, green, and purple grain streaks.
*   **Properties**: High moisture resistance and durability; approx 0.65 g/cm³.
*   **STEM Connection**: Study of anthocyanins and plant pigments that create the unique blue grain.

### 2. Lignum Vitae (Guaiacum officinale)
*   **Status**: National Flower / National Tree.
*   **Habitat**: Dry, coastal areas of Jamaica.
*   **Anatomy**: The "Tree of Life." Densest and hardest wood in the world (approx 1.30 g/cm³).
*   **Industrial Use**: Self-lubricating properties due to high resin content. Historically used for ship propeller shaft bearings and ball-point pen tips.

### 3. Bauxite (Aluminum Ore)
*   **Geology**: Jamaica's primary mineral resource. A sedimentary rock formed from the weathering of limestone.
*   **Composition**: Rich in Aluminum oxides (Alumina).
*   **Processing**: Refinement via the Bayer Process into Alumina, then smelting into Aluminum.
*   **Economic Botany Connection**: Land reclamation projects following bauxite mining focus on re-introducing endemic species.
