### 1. The Chemistry of Limestone Aquifers
Groundwater in karst environments (like Jamaica's Cockpit Country) is characterized by high mineral concentrations due to the dissolution of the **White Limestone Group**.

#### Key Measurement Metrics:
*   **Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)**: A measure of the combined content of all inorganic and organic substances. High TDS in karst usually indicates elevated Calcium (Ca) and Magnesium (Mg).
*   **pH (Hydrogen Ion Concentration)**: Karst water typically ranges from 7.0 to 8.5 (slightly alkaline) due to the buffering effect of bicarbonate.
*   **Electrolytic Conductivity**: A proxy for salinity and mineral density. Used to detect saltwater intrusion in coastal aquifers (e.g., Green Grotto).

### 3. Lab Exercise: Aquifer Forensic Audit
1.  **Sample Collection**: Initialize the `Mg·12` plug-out.
2.  **Telemetry**: Monitor the `TDS (PPM)` and `Hardness` readouts.
3.  **Analysis**: High Hardness (>300 ppm) indicates a mature karst conduit system.
4.  **Anomaly Detection**: A sudden drop in pH below 6.5 indicates potential agricultural or industrial runoff contamination.
